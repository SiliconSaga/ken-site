Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Kenneth running for Township Council?

Kenneth is the third generation of his family to own a home in West Orange and is running because he shares the same concerns he hears from residents every day — rising taxes, declining trust in town government, struggling small businesses, and decisions that don't reflect resident priorities. He's not running to be a politician. He's running to be a strong, qualified voice for the people who actually live here.

What makes Kenneth different from other candidates?

Kenneth is a Certified Public Accountant (CPA), Chartered Global Management Accountant (CGMA), and holds a Master's in Taxation. He has 30+ years of professional experience auditing organizations, identifying wasteful spending, strengthening internal controls, and building responsible budgets. He brings the exact skill set local government most often lacks: disciplined financial oversight.

What is Kenneth's stance on property taxes?

Property taxes in West Orange are unsustainably high and hurting families, seniors, and small businesses. Kenneth will push for line-by-line review of the municipal budget, identify and eliminate wasteful spending, demand multi-year fiscal planning, and oppose decisions that quietly add to long-term debt.

Where does Kenneth stand on overdevelopment and PILOT agreements?

Kenneth supports responsible growth that strengthens the tax base — not overdevelopment that overburdens schools, neighborhoods, traffic, or open space. PILOT (Payment In Lieu Of Taxes) agreements should be evaluated transparently with full disclosure of long-term impact on the schools and homeowners. Poorly structured PILOTs that benefit developers at residents' expense should be rejected.

How will Kenneth support West Orange small businesses?

Kenneth will push to establish a Business Concierge Service at Town Hall — a single point of help for new and existing businesses. He'll work to streamline licensing and permitting, review burdensome ordinances like the mercantile license requirement, and treat small business owners as partners in our recovery, not problems to regulate.

What does "transparency" mean in practice?

It means resident-friendly summaries of the budget and audits, clear publication of major contracts and PILOT terms, accessible explanations of major decisions before they're voted on, and a real council process where resident questions matter. Trust starts with communication.

Why does professional auditing experience matter in local government?

Most council decisions are financial decisions in disguise — contracts, budgets, debt, development incentives, settlements. Auditors are trained to read past the headline number, identify weak controls, evaluate risk, and ask the questions that protect taxpayers. That's exactly the discipline West Orange needs at the council table.
