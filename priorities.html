Priorities for West Orange

S.M.A.R.T. Leadership. Real Solutions.

I'm not running to be a politician. I'm running to listen, to be a strong voice for our community, and to bring real accountability to Town Hall.

Residents deserve a council member who hears their concerns, asks the harder questions, and demands honest answers about how their tax dollars are spent.

What I'm Hearing from Residents

A community proud to call home — with real concerns that demand real solutions.

West Orange is a community we're proud to call home — with strong neighborhoods and incredible resources like the South Mountain Reservation. But residents consistently raise key concerns:

  • Affordability challenges driven by rising property taxes and cost of living
  • Struggles facing small businesses
  • Lack of transparency and trust in local government
  • Stronger support for seniors and families with special needs
  • Quality-of-life concerns, including roads, traffic, and neighborhood safety
  • Thoughtful planning that protects neighborhoods from overdevelopment

I will be both a listening ear and a strong voice for our community.

We need real solutions rooted in nearly six decades of community experience and 30 years of financial expertise.

The S.M.A.R.T. Framework

Five Commitments. One Standard.

S.M.A.R.T. is how I'll govern: save taxpayer dollars, manage the town like a business, audit every major decision, strengthen our roads and neighborhoods, and deliver real transparency.

S
Save

Keeping West Orange Affordable

One of the biggest concerns I hear from residents is simple: West Orange is becoming too expensive. As a CPA and Auditor — and a lifelong resident raising the fourth generation of my family here — I know government has a responsibility to maximize the value of every taxpayer dollar.

Top priorities:

  • Fighting to reduce the property tax burden.
  • Identifying efficiencies and eliminating waste.
  • Strengthening financial oversight to ensure taxpayer dollars are spent wisely.
See more priorities
  • Promoting fiscally responsible budgets.
  • Avoiding unnecessary long-term debt that places a greater burden on future taxpayers.
  • Carefully reviewing major development proposals, including Prism, Rock Springs, and Stonehill, to ensure they are in the best interests of West Orange residents.
  • Evaluating PILOT agreements to ensure they fairly balance economic development while protecting taxpayers and our schools.
  • Helping seniors remain in the community they helped build.
  • Keeping West Orange affordable for families, young professionals, and future generations.

Every dollar saved at Town Hall is a dollar that stays in your pocket. Together, through responsible financial leadership and independent oversight, we can build a more affordable West Orange.

M
Manage

Business-Minded Leadership

Good government begins with good management. For almost four decades I've served in leadership roles with some of the world's largest advisory, accounting, and tax firms — and as a local small business owner, I understand firsthand the challenges business owners face.

Top priorities:

  • Applying the same financial leadership and management principles I've used throughout my career.
  • Streamlining licensing, permitting, and regulations to reduce unnecessary bureaucracy while maintaining appropriate oversight.
  • Establishing a Business Concierge Service at Town Hall to help entrepreneurs and business owners navigate licensing, permitting, and Township services.
See more priorities
  • Supporting local small businesses because they create jobs, strengthen our local economy, revitalize our commercial districts, and broaden our commercial tax base.
  • Improving efficiency through long-term planning, measurable goals, and accountability for results.
  • Making data-driven decisions that focus on long-term value for taxpayers rather than short-term politics.
  • Managing Township government with the same fiscal discipline, responsiveness, and customer service that residents and businesses expect from well-run organizations.

A well-managed government delivers better services, supports a stronger local economy, and makes better use of every taxpayer dollar.

A
Audit

Deliver Accountability

Every candidate promises accountability — my campaign begins with the profession that delivers it. I'm running as a CPA and Auditor with nearly four decades of independent financial oversight. An audit isn't about finding fault; it's about asking the right questions, protecting taxpayer dollars, and identifying risks before they become costly problems.

Top priorities:

  • Promoting clear and transparent financial reporting.
  • Carefully reviewing budgets and major expenditures before taxpayer dollars are spent.
  • Carefully reviewing development proposals and PILOT agreements to protect taxpayers and our schools.
See more priorities
  • Supporting responsible development that strengthens our tax base without overburdening schools, neighborhoods, or open space.
  • Asking the hard questions before major financial decisions are made.
  • Strengthening independent oversight and accountability throughout Township government.

Accountability isn't just something I talk about — it's what I've done professionally for nearly four decades. The best decisions begin with asking the right questions.

R
Roads

Safety & Quality of Life — Strong Neighborhoods

The quality of life in West Orange begins with strong neighborhoods. As a lifelong resident, the primary caregiver for my mother, and the father of a daughter with special needs, I understand how much safe streets, accessible infrastructure, and strong support matter to families here.

Top priorities:

  • Improving road maintenance through long-term planning and responsible infrastructure investment.
  • Enhancing pedestrian safety, traffic management, and neighborhood accessibility.
  • Supporting our police officers and firefighters who protect our community every day.
See more priorities
  • Improving accessibility for seniors, residents with disabilities, and families with special needs.
  • Addressing neighborhood concerns such as parking, traffic congestion, and quality-of-life issues through practical, common-sense solutions.
  • Protecting parks, open space, and the character of every neighborhood.
  • Preserving and improving the quality of life that makes West Orange a great place to live, work, and raise a family.

Strong neighborhoods build strong communities. Together, we can make every neighborhood safer, stronger, and an even better place to call home.

T
Transparency

Shine a Light on West Orange Finances

Transparency isn't a campaign slogan for me — it's been my profession. For nearly four decades as a CPA and Auditor, my job has been to ask the hard questions, verify the facts, strengthen accountability, and protect the public interest. The word "Public" in Certified Public Accountant isn't there by accident.

Top priorities:

  • Promoting open and accessible government.
  • Providing clear, understandable financial reporting for residents.
  • Improving communication between Township government and the public.
See more priorities
  • Encouraging greater public participation in local government.
  • Strengthening accountability through independent oversight.
  • Shining a light on West Orange finances so residents understand how their tax dollars are being spent.

Transparency builds trust, and an informed public is the foundation of accountable government.
