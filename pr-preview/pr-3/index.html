Why This Race Matters

This Race Is About Trust, Taxes, and Accountability

West Orange is a community we are proud to call home. But residents are raising serious concerns about rising property taxes, affordability, business pressure, transparency in government, overdevelopment, and everyday quality-of-life issues that too often go unresolved.

Kenneth Gibbons is running because residents deserve leadership that understands the numbers, asks harder questions, and puts the community before insiders and politics as usual.

What residents are telling us

  • Rising property taxes & affordability
  • Small businesses being squeezed out
  • Lack of trust and transparency at Town Hall
  • Overdevelopment & PILOT agreements
  • Roads, traffic, parking, and rodents
  • Support for seniors and special-needs families

The Framework

S.M.A.R.T. Leadership for West Orange

More than a campaign slogan — it is the framework that guides every decision.

S

Save

Keep West Orange affordable by reducing the property tax burden, eliminating waste, improving efficiency, promoting fiscally responsible budgets, and maximizing every taxpayer dollar.

M

Manage

Bring business-minded financial leadership, strategic planning, operational efficiency, and common-sense management to Township government.

A

Audit

Strengthen financial oversight by asking the hard questions, verifying the facts, identifying financial risks, carefully reviewing spending, and promoting accountability through independent oversight.

R

Roads, Safety & Quality of Life

Invest in roads and infrastructure, support first responders, address neighborhood concerns, support local businesses, and improve quality of life.

T

Transparency

Promote open government through clear communication, accessible financial information, and accountability that earns the public's trust.

Accountability Agenda

Let's Shine a Light on West Orange's Finances

A serious oversight plan from a CPA, auditor, and lifelong resident — built to protect taxpayers and rebuild trust.

  • Eliminate wasteful spending and demand line-by-line budget review
  • Strengthen financial oversight and internal controls
  • Carefully evaluate every PILOT for impact on schools and homeowners
  • Push for plain-English financial reporting residents can actually read
  • Support only responsible development that doesn't burden neighborhoods
  • Make resident questions a real part of the council process
