About Kenneth Gibbons

A Lifetime Dedicated to West Orange

Certified Public Accountant. Third generation of his family to own a home in West Orange. A candidate who brings real-world fiscal expertise to fight for our community.

My name is Kenneth Gibbons, and I'm proud to be the third generation of my family to own a home in West Orange — a lifelong resident of nearly six decades. Today, my daughter, Caroline, represents the fourth generation of our family to call West Orange our home.

Why I'm running

I'm running for West Orange Township Council to be a strong advocate for residents and small businesses and to help restore the community I grew up in — a town once known for:

Affordable property taxes
Thriving, family-owned businesses
Strong support for seniors, special-needs families, and essential community services for children
Fair, common-sense ordinances that support residents and small businesses — not burden them

Professional background

I am a Certified Public Accountant (CPA), Chartered Global Management Accountant (CGMA), and hold a Master's Degree in Taxation (MST) from Seton Hall University. For more than 30 years, I've advised individuals and businesses in accounting, finance, and taxation — helping them strengthen their financial position and plan for the future.

My experience includes:

  • Developing strategies to reduce tax burdens for individuals and businesses
  • Auditing organizations to identify internal control weaknesses, assess financial operations, and ensure transparent and accurate financial reporting
  • Identifying and eliminating wasteful spending
  • Improving efficiency and financial oversight
  • Building responsible, sustainable budgets

I've held leadership roles at major advisory, accounting, and taxation firms, bringing a collaborative, solutions-driven approach to complex challenges. I also hold a certificate in Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP) from the NLP Center of New York City, further strengthening my communication and problem-solving skills. I own a full-service CPA advisory, accounting, and taxation firm right here in town.

Why It Matters

Why professional financial oversight matters in town government

Most council decisions are financial decisions. West Orange deserves a council member trained to read past the surface — and protect taxpayers in the room where decisions get made.

Ask the Harder Questions

Auditors are trained to look past the headline number. Kenneth will press on assumptions, surface hidden costs, and dig into variances before they become surprises.

Spot Financial Weakness Early

Identifying weak internal controls and procurement gaps is everyday work for an auditor — and exactly what protects taxpayers in local government.

Strengthen Oversight

Bring professional standards of review to council deliberations on contracts, debt, settlements, and major development decisions.

Evaluate Real Long-Term Risk

Decisions like PILOTs and large developments carry decade-long consequences. Kenneth will weigh that risk seriously — not approve and apologize later.
